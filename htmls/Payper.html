WHAT IS PAYPERTM ?

Payper™ is a tradable financial digital asset.
The Payper™ is based on Bitcoin, Ether, other cryptocurrency and crypto indices created by PayperEx.

There are two types of Paypers BULL Payper™ and BEAR Payper™
If you expect that the price of the underlying asset will go up buy a BULL Payper™
If you expect that the price of the underlying asset will go down buy a BEAR Payper™

A limited amount of Paypers will be offered for sale.
The use of Blockchain technology ensures that the amount wouldn’t be diluted.
Initially, the Paypers will be sold in the IPS and then on PayperEx trading network.

The IPS (Initial Payper sale) – investors have the first opportunity to buy the Paypers before it will be traded on the network. IPS participants get up to 50% discount and more benefits.

PayperEx trading network – investors can buy Paypers from each other. Paypers value will be determined by the supply and demand.

HOW TO PROFIT FROM PAYPERTM

  1. Buy Low - Sell High

    When a Payper™ you own appreciates in value, in other words when the Payper™ price goes up, which means people are willing to pay you more for your Payper™ than you paid for them.

  2. Hedging

    A hedge is an investment to reduce the risk of adverse price movements in an asset. Normally, a hedge consists of taking an offsetting position in a related asset.

  3. Fee share

    Profit from just holdings the Payper™, a portion of the fees collected will be paid to you as a Payper™ holder in the form of fee share.

WHAT IS FEE-SHARE ?

A portion of the collected fees from each transaction in a specific Payper™ that will distributed to the Payper™ holders.

The Fee-Share will have distributed to the Payper™ holders according to the relative holding percentage of each one of them.

The Fee-Share will be paid when the underlying asset price will hit the strike price.

The first strike price will be announced before the Payper™ will start to be tradable, after that a new strike price will be determined immediately after every Fee-Share distributed.

The correlation between the Payper™ to the underlying assets is in this
Fee-Share structure.
